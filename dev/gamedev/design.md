# Top level ideas

## People

### Lessons from Tim Cain
realism isnt what ppl mean when they say 'this doesnt feel realistic'
  their problem with the game is that the game isnt self consistent

## Games

### Lessons from Caravan

dialog doesnt matter unless your super good at stories and have something compelling

if you have incremental quests, then give incremental rewards.
  paths of exploration should not yield 0 rewards

if you have core mechanics, either make them available right away, or if they require material to unlock, ensure that there are many many ways to get the material without many paths yield 0 rewards

### Lessons from Monster Hunter
Weighty movement makes moving and position character very intensional
no lock on to enemy means you have to aim your character manually before attacking
  more strategy and staying calm under pressure is important

more detailed models and style isnt always better
  if the style makes the game less clear then it makes the experience worse
    example: vines can be climbed in this game, but you cant see the vines bcuz of how detailed everything is
    the hack that makes it work in this game is that there are very limited maps, so you just memorize where the vines are
      which is another bad design (limited maps) helping this bad design bcuz workable

### Lessons from Atlyss
dont sweat the small details
  its ok to:
    character clipping slightly through pots
    for blinking - just squash the texture
    stretching and squashing and rotate with no more than simple tweens is good enough alot of times
hover over an icon (or being focused on an icon) for a couple seconds brings up a tooltip
  tooltips can work not just on mouse hovers, but on all controller schemes

### Lessons from Lil Gator Game
UI styles like the pause menu having consistency with the rest of the game can sell the style or vibe of the game
  the UI is very playful, almost like a kids planner with stickers, crayon based text, etc

### Lessons from Atelier game series
hitting a niche can be a good long term play
having very consistent (average yearly) releases can keep you relavent and build up a cult following that will keep you around
Atelier has been doing this for 30 years
  cozy rpg - alchemy in crafting stuff from basic real world ingredients
great music
anime style
